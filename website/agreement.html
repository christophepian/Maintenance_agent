Preamble
The Company is developing a property management platform targeting Swiss landlords and property managers. The Company wishes to raise a small amount from its close network to fund early operations and product development prior to a formal seed financing round. The Investor wishes to provide a loan on the terms set out below, with the option to convert into equity at a later date.
1 — Loan Amount
1.1
The Investor agrees to lend to the Company the principal amount of CHF  amount  (the "Principal"), subject to a minimum of CHF 100 and the aggregate pool cap set out in clause 6.
1.2
The Principal shall be transferred by the Investor to the Company's designated bank account within 10 business days of the date of this Agreement. The Company will provide payment details separately by email.
1.3
The loan is made in Swiss Francs (CHF). No foreign currency risk applies.
2 — Interest
2.1
The Principal shall bear interest at the rate of 5% per annum, calculated as simple interest (not compounded) from the date the Principal is received by the Company.
2.2
Interest accrues daily on the outstanding Principal. No interest payments are made periodically; accrued interest is paid (or converted) only at Maturity in accordance with clause 4.
2.3
For the avoidance of doubt, if the Investor elects to convert under clause 4.2, accrued interest is forgiven and does not form part of the conversion calculation — the conversion is based solely on the Principal.
3 — Maturity
3.1
This loan matures automatically upon the closing of the Company's first Qualified Financing, defined as a priced equity round with aggregate proceeds of at least CHF 500,000 from one or more institutional or professional investors (the "Maturity Event").
3.2
If no Maturity Event has occurred within 36 months of the date of this Agreement (the "Long-Stop Date"), either party may declare the loan due and payable by providing 30 days' written notice, in which case clause 4.3 applies.
3.3
The Company shall notify the Investor in writing within 5 business days of the closing of a Qualified Financing.
4 — Conversion and Repayment at Maturity
4.1
Within 30 days of receiving written notice of the Maturity Event under clause 3.3, the Investor shall notify the Company in writing of their election under clause 4.2 or 4.3. If no election is received within this period, the Investor shall be deemed to have elected repayment under clause 4.3.
4.2
Option A — Conversion into equity. The Investor may elect to convert the Principal into shares of the Company at a price per share equal to the price paid by investors in the Qualified Financing multiplied by (1 − 30%) = 0.70 (the "Conversion Price"). The number of shares issued shall be:
Shares = Principal ÷ Conversion Price
Shares issued on conversion shall be of the same class and rank as the shares issued to investors in the Qualified Financing, subject to any rights attaching to that class. The Company shall take all corporate steps necessary to issue such shares promptly following the election.
4.3
Option B — Cash repayment. The Investor may elect to receive repayment of the Principal plus all accrued and unpaid interest calculated pursuant to clause 2. The Company shall make such payment within 30 business days of receiving the election notice (or of the deemed election under clause 4.1).

5 — Use of Proceeds
5.1
The Company shall use the proceeds from this loan for general corporate purposes, including but not limited to product development, sales and marketing activities, and working capital. The Company shall not use the proceeds to repay existing shareholder loans or make distributions.
6 — Aggregate Pool Cap
6.1
The aggregate principal amount raised by the Company under this instrument (across all investors) shall not exceed CHF 75,000 (the "Pool Cap"). This cap is set so that all investors who convert under clause 4.2 collectively hold no more than approximately 5% of the Company's fully-diluted share capital at conversion, assuming a Qualified Financing at a CHF 2,000,000 pre-money valuation.
6.2
If accepting this investment would cause the aggregate to exceed the Pool Cap, the Company may, at its option, (a) decline the investment or (b) accept only the portion that fits within the remaining capacity, with the Investor's written consent.
7 — Representations and Warranties
7.1
By the Company. The Company represents that: (a) it is duly formed and validly existing under Swiss law; (b) it has the corporate authority to enter into this Agreement; (c) the execution and performance of this Agreement do not violate any other agreement to which the Company is a party; and (d) no insolvency proceedings have been commenced against it.
7.2
By the Investor. The Investor represents that: (a) they have the legal capacity and authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) they are investing on their own behalf; (c) they have read and understood the risk warning in clause 8; and (d) the amount invested represents funds they can afford to lose.
8 — Risk Acknowledgement
8.1
The Investor acknowledges that: (a) the Company is an early-stage venture and there is a material risk of total loss of the Principal; (b) repayment under clause 4.3 depends on the Company having sufficient cash at maturity, which is not guaranteed; (c) conversion under clause 4.2 results in shares in a private, illiquid company with no guaranteed future value or exit; and (d) no prospectus has been prepared and no regulatory approval has been obtained in connection with this offer.
8.2
This Agreement is offered privately to individuals personally known to the founders and does not constitute a public offer of securities under the Swiss Financial Services Act (FinSA) or any other applicable law.
9 — Confidentiality
9.1
Each party agrees to keep the existence and terms of this Agreement confidential and not to disclose them to any third party without the prior written consent of the other party, except: (a) to professional advisers bound by confidentiality; (b) as required by applicable law or regulatory authority; or (c) in connection with the enforcement of this Agreement.
10 — Transfer and Assignment
10.1
The Investor may not assign or transfer their rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company. The Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or corporate restructuring, provided that the assignee assumes all obligations hereunder.
11 — Miscellaneous
11.1
Entire agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions and understandings.
11.2
Amendments. Any amendment to this Agreement must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
11.3
Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid. The remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
11.4
Waiver. No failure or delay in exercising any right under this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of that right.
11.5
Notices. Notices under this Agreement shall be sent by email to the addresses stated at signing, with written confirmation requested.
12 — Governing Law and Jurisdiction
12.1
This Agreement is governed by Swiss law, in particular the Swiss Code of Obligations (OR). The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Canton of Vaud, Switzerland.
12.2
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall constitute an original. Signed PDF copies transmitted by email shall have the same legal effect as originals.
