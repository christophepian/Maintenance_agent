The Problem

The problem in one sentence

Switzerland's 341,000 private landlords must choose between paying a régie 4–7% of their rent for generic service, or self-managing with spreadsheets and no safety net.

The solution in one sentence

We give them a third option: a platform that automates compliance, guides day-to-day decisions, and — as their portfolio grows — delivers the intelligence that private banks provide for equities.

The dilemma every small landlord faces
Delegate to a régie and lose 4–7% of rent. Self-manage and drown in complexity.

According to UBS (December 2025), professional property management fees in Switzerland run between 4–5% of rental income for a fully occupied building — rising to 7% in lower-rent regions. On a building with 12 apartments at CHF 1,500/mo, that is CHF 900 per month, or CHF 10,800 per year, extracted directly from yield. For a small landlord, that is not a service fee. It is a meaningful reduction in return on a long-term asset.

Strategic — forward-looking Layer 3
06
Rent adjustment — legal minefield

Swiss rent law ties increases to the reference mortgage rate, inflation index, and cost increases — with specific forms, notice periods, and cantonal variations. Most landlords either never adjust or adjust incorrectly. Both outcomes cost real money.

07
Financial forecasting

Projecting net cashflows 3–5 years out — modelling rent increases, vacancy assumptions, maintenance reserves. Without this, every major decision (refinancing, sale, renovation) is made blind. No landlord today has a living financial model of their portfolio.

08
Cashflow planning for renovations

Major capex — roofing, heating systems, façade renovation — arrives predictably but lands as a surprise. Landlords have no forward-looking replacement schedule, no capital reserve logic, and no way to time interventions against rent cycles or financing conditions.

09
ESG & compliance future-proofing

Cantonal energy requirements are tightening. Minergie standards, mandatory energy certificates, and upcoming retrofit obligations will create legal exposure and asset depreciation for unprepared owners. Today's self-managers have no roadmap for this transition.

Financial performance — current state Layer 2
05
No performance visibility

Is the property performing well? What is the actual net yield after maintenance and vacancy? How does it compare to similar buildings in the same canton? No self-managing landlord today has a clear answer to these questions. They know their gross rent. Everything else is a guess.

Operational — day-to-day Layer 1
01
Document & correspondence management

Lease agreements, entry/exit protocols, cantonal forms, insurance documents, maintenance records, tax-relevant receipts. Stored across email folders, a shared drive, and a desk drawer. When a tenant disputes something or the tax office requests records, finding the right document is a crisis.

02
Maintenance requests & tenant communication

Requests arrive by WhatsApp, email, or phone call. No structured intake, no priority triage, no audit trail. When something goes wrong — a boiler failure at 7pm on a Friday — the landlord has no vetted contractor list, no protocol, and full personal liability for response time.

03
Tradesperson coordination

Finding a plumber, electrician, or heating engineer on short notice, getting quotes, comparing costs, authorising work, tracking invoices — each intervention is a small project. Most landlords have no vetted contractor network, no pricing benchmarks, and no way to know if they overpaid.

04
QR invoices & rent tracking

Mandatory since 2022, the QR-invoice format is unfamiliar to most private landlords. Generating compliant invoices, tracking payments, following up on arrears — all done manually today via e-banking exports and spreadsheets. One missed payment, no alert.

The constraint we are designing around
Owners can't easily leave their régie — so we don't ask them to.

Discovery interviews confirm it: even dissatisfied owners hesitate to switch. Contract notice periods of 3–12 months, fear of operational gaps, and years of established inertia create real switching cost resistance. The conventional SaaS playbook — "sign up, migrate, cancel your old provider" — faces structural friction in this market.

The path of least resistance
Enter where there is no friction: the owner's financial picture.

Régie reports are sent to owners monthly — dense PDFs that provide little clarity on actual performance. We ingest those reports, transform them into a financial dashboard with NOI analysis, benchmarks, and forward scenarios, and charge for that intelligence layer. The régie does not need to cooperate. The owner does not need to switch. We earn recurring revenue from day one.

Moat & Defensibility

01
Swiss Compliance DNA
QR-invoice (SIX standard), cantonal Formular B variants (26 cantons), FADP/nLPD data residency, trilingual UX baked in from day one. US and European players cannot replicate this without 2–3 years of regulatory engineering. Every month of early presence hardens this advantage.
02
Proprietary Maintenance Data
Every maintenance request, contractor invoice, and cost event logged on the platform builds a picture that does not exist anywhere today: what a boiler replacement actually costs in canton Vaud, which contractors overcharge, what normal annual maintenance spend looks like for a 1960s building in Geneva. After three years across thousands of properties, we can tell a landlord whether their costs are in line with comparable buildings — or whether their régie is spending 40% above market. No competitor can replicate this without three years of operations. It cannot be bought.
03
Switching Cost Architecture
Every month on our platform, a client's building history, signed leases, Nebenkosten history, and maintenance records accumulate. Switching means losing years of compliance documentation. We design data portability for trust — but the cognitive cost of switching rises steeply after 12 months of use.
04
Network: Contractor Graph
A verified Swiss artisan directory — rated, canton-specific, specialisation-tagged — is valuable only when dense. We build it systematically from Phase III. Once 500+ contractors are active, every new client immediately benefits from the network, and every contractor has incentive to stay on our platform.
05
Regulatory Timing
The CO₂ Act creates mandatory renovation documentation requirements for all building owners by 2030. We will be 5+ years into building the compliance infrastructure before the regulatory deadline arrives. First-mover advantage in mandatory compliance is an exceptionally durable position.
06
Distribution Asymmetry
Landlords who grow their portfolio upgrade to higher-tier plans — and generate referrals within their landlord networks. Small régies that professionalise move to mid-market plans. Every phase feeds the next without a cold-start problem. The product ladder maps directly to our customers' own ambitions — we grow with them.
