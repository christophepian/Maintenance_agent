Instrument terms

The instrument is a pre-incorporation convertible loan agreement (CLA) governed by Swiss OR. No shares are issued today — investors hold a right to convert into equity at the next qualified financing round, at a discount to the round price.

How it works

From investment to equity — three paths.

The convertible loan has a clear, predictable lifecycle. Here is how your investment moves from signed agreement to equity.

1
You sign a Convertible Loan Agreement and transfer principal
A bilateral CLA is signed between you and Sencilo (pre-incorporation: between you and the founding entity). Your principal — minimum CHF 25K — is transferred to the company account. You hold a contractual right to future conversion; no shares are issued at this stage. The agreement is governed by Swiss OR.
Day 0
2
Qualified financing triggers automatic conversion
If Sencilo closes a priced equity round of CHF 500K or more within the 24-month window, your loan converts automatically into shares of the same class as the lead investor — at the lower of: the cap-implied price (CHF 2.0M pre-money / total shares) or the round price less 20% discount. The planned seed round is CHF 500K at CHF 2.0M pre-money: at that price, cap and discounted price are equal, giving early investors parity with seed investors at minimum. No further action required from you.
Path A — Conversion on financing
3
Long-stop reached — you choose your outcome
If no qualified financing has occurred after 24 months, the long-stop provisions activate. You have two options: (a) request repayment of your principal in full, or (b) elect conversion at the cap-implied price (CHF 2.0M pre-money valuation), acquiring equity without needing a third-party round. This choice is yours — the company cannot force either outcome unilaterally.
Path B — Long-stop investor election
Investor protection

24-month long-stop — your choice at maturity.

Unlike hard mandatory-repayment instruments, this structure gives investors genuine optionality at the long-stop. You are never forced into an outcome that doesn't serve your interests.

🔄
Option A — Repayment
Request full repayment of your principal. Sencilo is contractually obligated to return your CHF investment within a defined repayment window (typically 60–90 days of notice). No conversion, no dilution, no lock-in beyond maturity.
Capital protection
📈
Option B — Convert at cap
Elect to convert your principal into equity at the cap valuation (CHF 2.0M pre-money), regardless of whether a formal financing round has occurred. You become a shareholder at the price agreed on day one — useful if the company is growing but hasn't yet closed a priced round.
